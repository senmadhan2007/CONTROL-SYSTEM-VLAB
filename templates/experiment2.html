Aim

To identify and study the damping characteristics of a second order control system by analyzing its time response and determining whether the system is underdamped, critically damped, or overdamped.

Apparatus Required

  • Computer / Laptop
  • Scilab Software
  • Internet Browser
  • Virtual Control System Laboratory

Theory

A second order control system is commonly represented by the standard transfer function:

G(s) = ωn2 / (s2 + 2ζωns + ωn2)

Here, ωn is the natural frequency and ζ (zeta) is the damping ratio of the system. The damping ratio determines the nature of the system response.

  • Underdamped (0 < ζ < 1): Oscillatory response with decreasing amplitude.
  • Critically damped (ζ = 1): Fastest response without oscillations.
  • Overdamped (ζ > 1): Slow response without oscillations.

By observing the system response such as overshoot, settling time, and oscillations, the damping condition of the second order system can be identified.
